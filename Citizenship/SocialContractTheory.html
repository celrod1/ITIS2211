Social Contract Theory

     Social Contract Theory is a morality based upon a set behavioral rules that rational people will abide on the condition that other rational actors in society will also abide them so that everyone may benefit. It assumes that other citizens in society will act in good-faith but also presumes enforceability of these rules in the event of others' non-compliance. A basic principle of Social Contract Theory is the notion that everyone's interests are best promoted through cooperation and adherence to rules of moral conduct.

     Compassionate Critical Thinking harmonizes with this basic principle only partly: while it does promote cooperation, it does not maintain doing the right thing simple means "following the rules" without exception. Instead, it questions rules and seeks alternative cases where rules can be shown to be at odds with the interests of vulnerable groups -- this is the "Critical Thinking" aspect of Compassionate Critical Thinking. The social institution of Public Education could encourage substantial human progress by promoting the development of Critical Thinking skills.
