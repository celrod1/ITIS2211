Compassionate Critical Thinking/Wisdom

     The proliferation of [communication/social media platforms] in the digital has facilitated rapid and widespread sharing of information. While "free and open sharing of information" sounds like a noble achievement, in recent years cracks have begun to show. As these information technologies have matured, users and developers have sought to maximize viewership which has led to increasing polarization within civil societies. Indeed, these platforms have shifted from "information" sharing to "content" sharing -- greater value has been placed on "viewership" than the integrity and veracity of shared information.

     Furthermore, the algorithms deployed on these platforms have led to "filter bubbles" and "information silos" that have exacerbated divisions in society. Filter bubbles can be understood as algorithmic recommendations/displays to users according to their associated engagement with prior content. "Information silos" are another consequence of these algorithms: users have been unknowingly co-opted into networks of [similar] users who hold similar views to their own, creating an echo-chamber of apparent consensus while providing few avenues for engaging with other users outside of their invisible network who hold critical or alternative views to their own.

     We propose that 'compassionate critical thinking' is a worthwhile trait that every citizen in society ought to possess. We 'define a compassionate critical thinker' as someone who is sensitive to the welfare of all living things, seeks Truth with an open mind, and recognizes that other humans in society are fundamentally acting in good faith, harbor no ill-will toward them, and are allies who mean them no harm. We claim that if everyone possessed and promoted this virtue, then humanity would be improved. We note that 'possessing this virtue' implies that the practitioner is sensitive and self-aware to new opportunities to consistently manifest this virtue through their behavior and attitudes toward other humans in society.

     We will examine this claim through the lens of eight ethical systems and respond to any challenges or agreements that arise throughout this tour. We will explain how actively practicing 'compassionate critical thinking' remedies many problems related to: information vacuums, the formation of filter bubbles and information silos, willful ignorance of in-groups, hostility and bigotry toward sub-groups in society, nationalism, xenophobia, and stochastic terrorism toward the 'perceived villains' of in-groups. We will also explain how several social institutions could be modified or embraced to promote this trait.
