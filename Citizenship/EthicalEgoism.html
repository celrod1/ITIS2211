Ethical Egoism

     Ethical Egoism places self-interest above all and contends that actions are "right" if they provide personal benefit. Ethical Egoism can justify treating other people poorly as long as they can provide no benefit. It views the human interests as a zero-sum game where only one winner may prevail. In personal interactions, this perspective precludes any faith in the goodwill of others since others want to "make you lose" so that they can "win". Thus, Ethical Egoism defends the "morality of selfishness." Altruism (in the purest sense) is viewed as a weakness and lost opportunity for personal gain.

     Compassionate Critical Thinking contends that the interests of others does matter: it does not divide the world into two groups of people with competing interests (ourselves or our "tribe" vs. everyone else). Unlike Ethical Egoism (which justifies treating people badly if it is in the actor's self-interest), Compassionate Critical Thinkers follow the Golden Rule and treat others how they wish to be treated.
