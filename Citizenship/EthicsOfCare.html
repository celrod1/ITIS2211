Ethics Of Care / Feminism

     The primary concern of Feminism recognizing and understanding the underlying sources or causes of gender inequality in society so that solutions for improvement can be proposed and developed to better promote gender equality. A basic claim of Feminism is that females possess a fundamentally different orientation toward the world relative to the dominant ethical systems put forward by men. As a philosophical system, Feminism informs us that different moral judgements may be reached according to the orientation and priority of differing (gender) values. Feminine ethical judgments and deliberations are distinct because they often emphasize an "ethic of care" that demonstrates compassion toward others and consideration of their concerns when evaluating ethical dilemmas.

     Compassionate Critical Thinking is at home with 'the Ethics of Care'. CCT involves setting aside one's own values and beliefs so that we may be more receptive toward understanding and recognizing the legitimate values and concerns of others. It has compassion for the inherent differences of human orientations by acknowledging everyone's personal struggle with the human condition. It empathizes with the individual struggles of every living Thing to preserve its own existence and defend against challenges to its survival in its own unique way. CCT does not claim to understand these struggles, but it does attempt to acknowledge them in the world. This does not imply that all of these concerns require impartiality: deeply personal relationships are incidental to the human condition; loyalty to friends and family is a virtue. Anthropology reveals a lengthy history of human kinship and tribalism that still exists today.
