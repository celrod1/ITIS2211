Cultural Relativism

     As an ethical system, Cultural Relativism contends that all cultures are different and there is no basis for the claim that any culture's moral codes are superior to others. Thus, no independent, objective standard is possible, and determinations of right and wrong are an extension of social norms and conventions. Under strict Cultural Relativism, universal moral principles are only possible through unanimous agreement of cultural values.

     Our claim that humanity would be improved if everyone practiced the trait of "compassionate critical thinking" withstands critiques from cultural relativism by definition: 'universal practice' implies that this trait is a universal cultural norm. Regardless, Cultural Relativism's basic principle of open-mindedness toward alternative beliefs is in accord with the principles of 'compassionate critical thinking'. Compassionate Critical Thinkers are careful to recognize the presence of their own biases while evaluating new ideas and beliefs with an open-mind. However, this does not imply that they withhold judgement or maintain limitless tolerance about the harm that certain beliefs or values may cause. Indeed, the human-element of well-being is central to their concerns.
