Virtue Ethics

     Virtue Ethics embraces a constellation of positive traits that encourage "good personhood" (thus citizenship). These positive traits are actualized through the consistent application of these qualities to do the "right" thing. It is a habitual mindset that is manifested through action. A person is "good" and "virtuous" through their sincere activity and mental attitudes in the world, not the self-righteous ideals in their head. The practice of CCT by all citizens in society is a virtue.

     We view CCT as a marriage between "Intellectual Virtues" and the virtues of Care and Compassion. CCT recognizes that the ability to communicate with nearly anyone, anywhere in the world can be valuable to the development of compassion and open-minded curiosity. It acknowledges the vast disconnect between 'online personas' and the humans behind them. In its current form, social media has allowed modern societies to reduce human concerns to memes; to interact with other people as though they are unreal, virtual characters in a simulation (possessing no value).

     This aspect of social media has proved fertile ground for growing hostility and bigotry toward dehumanized groups of people in society. The recent surge of nationalism and xenophobia paired with willful ignorance and failure of compassion has led to a growing number of acts of stochastic terrorism against innocent people. CCT does acknowledge that compassion toward others who seek to harm us, as with any virtue, has its boundaries. However, sincere Compassionate Critical Thinking compels an individual to attempt to name and understand their own (as well as others') underlying fears and biases that may foster prejudice or foment information vacuums. CCT presumes that everyone is doing their best and attempts to apprehend when others are operating contrary to this assumption.
